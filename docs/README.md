# OctoAcme Project Management Documentation

Welcome to OctoAcme's Project Management documentation! This directory provides comprehensive guidance on how we plan, execute, and continuously improve our project delivery.

## Overview

OctoAcme's approach to project management centers on delivering customer value through iterative, evidence-driven work. Projects begin with a structured initiation process that ensures alignment among stakeholders, clear business objectives, and success metrics. Each project is provided a "one-pager" that defines the problem statement, intended outcomes, timeline, major risks, and team composition. The strong focus on up-front clarity allows teams to move from initiation to planning with a shared understanding of priorities, trade-offs, and how success will be measured.

Planning is a collaborative activity that breaks down initiatives into actionable backlogs, supported by well-defined acceptance criteria and estimates. OctoAcme emphasizes transparency through visual management tools like project boards, prioritization of dependencies and risks, and explicit Definition of Done criteria for each sprint or deliverable. Risk management is an ongoing process tracked in a centralized register, with regular reviews and escalation pathways defined to ensure timely mitigation. The workflow also supports robust change management via standardized templates for new features, releases, and incident resolutions.

Roles and responsibilities within OctoAcme projects are designed for clarity and accountability. Project Managers coordinate schedules, risk management, and communications, while Product Managers own the vision, priorities, and outcome measurement. Developers focus on solution implementation, quality, and maintainability, partnering closely with QA to validate that features meet acceptance standards. Stakeholders are involved through structured updates, milestone reviews, and clear escalation paths for rapid decision-making when issues arise.

Quality assurance is deeply embedded in the OctoAcme workflow. Automated testing, code reviews, continuous integration, and manual QA all play a role across the execution lifecycle. Before release, all acceptance criteria must be met, and deployments follow a thorough checklist—from CI passes and smoke tests to well-communicated stakeholder announcements and rollback planning. After a release or major milestone, retrospectives are held to capture learnings and drive a culture of continuous improvement. This commitment to reflection and iteration ensures that not only are deliverables high-quality, but teams themselves evolve best practices over time.
